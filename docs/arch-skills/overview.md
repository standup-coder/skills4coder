## Introduction

The Architect Skills Tree encompasses all the essential skills needed to become a successful technical architect or system designer. This guide organizes skills by architecture type and experience level, providing clear learning paths for architects at any stage of their career.

## Architecture Types

### Solution Architecture
Focuses on designing solutions that meet specific business requirements and technical constraints.

**Core Focus Areas**:
- **Requirements Analysis**: Translating business needs into technical solutions
- **System Integration**: Connecting disparate systems and technologies
- **Technology Selection**: Evaluating and recommending appropriate technologies
- **Solution Design**: Creating comprehensive architecture for specific projects
- **Vendor Management**: Evaluating and managing third-party technology vendors
- **Stakeholder Communication**: Communicating technical concepts to non-technical audiences

### Enterprise Architecture
Focuses on the overall technology strategy and standards for an organization.

**Core Focus Areas**:
- **Enterprise Strategy**: Aligning IT with business objectives
- **Governance**: Establishing standards, policies, and compliance
- **Portfolio Management**: Managing the technology portfolio across the organization
- **Standards Definition**: Creating and maintaining technology standards
- **Roadmapping**: Developing long-term technology roadmaps
- **Change Management**: Leading technology transformations across the organization

### Cloud Architecture
Focuses on designing and implementing cloud-native solutions.

**Core Focus Areas**:
- **Cloud Platforms**: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud Platform
- **Infrastructure as Code**: Terraform, CloudFormation, ARM Templates
- **Container Orchestration**: Kubernetes, ECS, AKS
- **Serverless Architecture**: Lambda, Azure Functions, Google Cloud Functions
- **Cloud Security**: Identity management, encryption, compliance
- **Cost Optimization**: Managing and optimizing cloud spending

### Security Architecture
Focuses on designing systems with security as a primary concern.

**Core Focus Areas**:
- **Security by Design**: Integrating security from the ground up
- **Identity and Access Management**: User authentication and authorization
- **Encryption and Key Management**: Protecting data at rest and in transit
- **Vulnerability Assessment**: Identifying and mitigating security risks
- **Compliance**: Ensuring adherence to regulations (SOX, GDPR, HIPAA)
- **Incident Response**: Developing and executing security response plans

### Data Architecture
Focuses on designing and managing data solutions across an organization.

**Core Focus Areas**:
- **Data Modeling**: Designing schemas and relationships
- **Data Integration**: ETL/ELT processes and data pipelines
- **Master Data Management**: Maintaining consistent, authoritative data
- **Data Governance**: Ensuring data quality, privacy, and compliance
- **Big Data Technologies**: Hadoop, Spark, NoSQL databases
- **Analytics Platforms**: BI tools, data warehousing, visualization

## Skill Levels

### Junior Architect
**Experience**: 1-3 years in technical leadership roles

**Core Skills**:
- Understanding of system design principles
- Ability to create basic architecture diagrams
- Knowledge of common architectural patterns
- Experience with technology evaluation
- Basic understanding of business requirements

### Mid-Level Architect
**Experience**: 3-5 years in architectural roles

**Core Skills**:
- Independent design of moderately complex systems
- Technology assessment and selection
- Stakeholder engagement and communication
- Designing for scalability and performance
- Risk assessment and mitigation

### Senior Architect
**Experience**: 5-8 years in architectural roles

**Core Skills**:
- Design of large-scale, complex systems
- Cross-team technical leadership
- Strategic technology planning
- Vendor and partner evaluation
- Technical roadmap development

### Principal/Lead Architect
**Experience**: 8+ years in architectural roles

**Core Skills**:
- Enterprise-wide architectural vision
- Organizational technology strategy
- Industry thought leadership
- Critical business decision making
- Mentoring and coaching of junior architects

## Cross-Cutting Architectural Concerns

### Scalability and Performance
- **Horizontal vs Vertical Scaling**: Understanding when to scale out vs up
- **Load Distribution**: Implementing load balancers and distributed systems
- **Caching Strategies**: Designing effective caching at multiple layers
- **Performance Monitoring**: Establishing performance baselines and alerts
- **Capacity Planning**: Forecasting resource needs and scaling requirements

### Reliability and Availability
- **Fault Tolerance**: Designing systems to handle component failures
- **Disaster Recovery**: Planning for and recovering from catastrophic events
- **High Availability**: Ensuring systems remain operational during failures
- **Backup and Restore**: Designing reliable backup and recovery procedures
- **Chaos Engineering**: Proactively testing system resilience

### Security and Compliance
- **Zero Trust Architecture**: Implementing security based on "never trust, always verify"
- **Data Privacy**: Ensuring compliance with privacy regulations
- **Security Testing**: Incorporating security into the SDLC
- **Penetration Testing**: Conducting and managing security assessments
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Understanding and implementing compliance requirements

### Cost Optimization
- **Resource Efficiency**: Optimizing cloud and infrastructure costs
- **Total Cost of Ownership**: Evaluating long-term costs of technical decisions
- **Cost Monitoring**: Implementing systems to track and alert on expenses
- **FinOps Practices**: Applying financial operations principles to technology
- **Budget Management**: Planning and managing technology budgets

## Architecture Design Process

### Requirements Gathering
- Business requirement analysis
- Technical constraint identification
- Stakeholder interview and workshop facilitation
- Requirements prioritization and categorization
- Gap analysis and assumption documentation

### Architecture Design
- High-level architecture patterns selection
- Technology stack evaluation and recommendation
- Component decomposition and interface design
- Data flow and interaction modeling
- Risk assessment and mitigation planning

### Documentation and Communication
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- System architecture diagrams (C4 model, etc.)
- Technical specifications and standards
- Presentation to technical and non-technical audiences
- Architecture review and approval processes

### Implementation and Evolution
- Technical guidance during implementation
- Architecture compliance checking
- Refinement based on implementation feedback
- Evolution planning for changing requirements
- Knowledge transfer and team enablement

## Technology Domains

### Infrastructure Technologies
- Networking: VPN, VPC, DNS, CDNs, load balancing
- Storage: Block, file, object storage solutions
- Compute: Virtual machines, containers, serverless platforms
- Security: Firewalls, VPNs, identity management systems
- Monitoring: Logging, metrics, alerting, APM tools

### Application Technologies
- Frameworks: Web frameworks, microservices frameworks
- Databases: SQL, NoSQL, graph, time-series databases
- Messaging: Queues, pub/sub, event streaming platforms
- APIs: REST, GraphQL, gRPC, SOAP
- Integration: ESB, ETL/ELT tools, API gateways

### Platform Technologies
- Container Orchestration: Kubernetes, Docker Swarm, ECS
- Service Mesh: Istio, Linkerd, Consul Connect
- CI/CD: Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions, ArgoCD
- Monitoring: Prometheus, Grafana, ELK stack, Datadog
- Security: Vault, Keycloak, OAuth/OIDC providers
